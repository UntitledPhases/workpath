**Recommendation**

Focus the next 30 days on **residential replacement leads**, with a tight offer that also attaches **maintenance plans and IAQ/efficiency add-ons** after diagnosis.

Reason: for a tiny HVAC/service contractor, the best 30-day bet is the segment with the shortest sales cycle, urgent seasonal demand, and highest near-term cash potential. Commercial maintenance contracts are strategically attractive, but slower to win. IAQ/energy-efficiency upsells are good margin, but they work best as an attachment to existing service and replacement calls, not as the main demand engine.

**Comparison Table**

| Segment | 30-day demand | Cash flow | Execution fit for tiny shop | Main risk | Rank |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|---:|

**Evidence Or Reasoning**

Residential replacement demand has mixed signals, but enough near-term pull to justify focus. ACHR News reports that replacement and service remain the core driver because over 90% of homes have installed AC or heat pump systems, while new construction is not the main engine. It also notes 2026 may be a cautious turnaround year, with replacement activity and existing home sales among the stronger demand sources. Source: [ACHR News](https://www.achrnews.com/articles/165674-signs-of-a-turnaround-what-2026-holds-for-the-hvac-construction-market).

There is also pressure on residential HVAC margins. EGIA's 2026 contractor survey highlights labor shortages, rising costs, regulation, and widespread price increases among contractors. That argues for disciplined lead qualification, not chasing every replacement lead. Source: [Contracting Business / EGIA survey coverage](https://www.contractingbusiness.com/industry-news/news/55373105/2026-hvac-survey-finds-rising-prices-and-ai-interest).

Energy-efficiency upgrades are credible add-ons: ENERGY STAR says heating and cooling are almost half of typical household energy use, and qualifying air-source heat pumps may be eligible for federal tax credits up to $2,000, depending on product and eligibility. Source: [ENERGY STAR air-source heat pumps](https://www.energystar.gov/products/air_source_heat_pumps).

Commercial maintenance is the best long-term asset, but the 30-day window works against it. ACCA frames commercial HVAC as a B2B market requiring different pricing, technical, people, and money management systems. That is worth building, but it is not usually the fastest path to booked revenue unless you already have warm commercial relationships. Source: [ACCA commercial HVAC](https://www.acca.org/members/industry/commercial-hvac).

**Risks**

The biggest risk with residential replacements is buying expensive leads that turn into price-shopping homeowners. Guard against this by prequalifying age of system, repair history, ownership status, financing need, and install timing before dispatching a senior tech or comfort advisor.

The second risk is install capacity. If you can only install one system per week, do not flood the calendar with low-quality estimates. Aim for fewer, better leads.

The third risk is trust. Many homeowners are skeptical of aggressive replacement selling. Lead with diagnosis, options, and repair-vs-replace economics. Use IAQ and efficiency add-ons only when the system condition supports them.

**Next Steps**

For the next 30 days, run a focused "replace before peak heat" campaign.

1. Pull your customer list for systems older than 10-12 years, repeat repairs, R-22/obsolete equipment, high utility complaints, weak cooling, and old maintenance-plan customers.
2. Call/text/email that list before buying cold leads. Offer a paid diagnostic or replacement consultation credited toward install.
3. Build a simple three-option quote: repair, standard replacement, high-efficiency/comfort upgrade.
4. Attach one maintenance plan to every replacement quote and one IAQ/efficiency option only where relevant: media filter, thermostat, duct sealing, surge protection, UV/air purifier, or heat pump upgrade.
5. Track only five numbers: leads, booked visits, close rate, average ticket, gross margin.
6. Set a stop-loss: if paid leads do not produce qualified appointments within 7-10 days, shift spend back to existing customers and referral outreach.

**Missing Context**

This recommendation could change if you already have a warm commercial pipeline, a strong maintenance salesperson, or limited install capacity. The deciding questions are: your average replacement gross margin, current install backlog, lead source cost, number of active maintenance customers, and whether you have any existing commercial relationships that can close without a long bid cycle.
