**Recommendation: focus the next 30 days on residential replacement leads.**

For a tiny HVAC/service contractor, this is the best near-term segment because it has the shortest path to booked revenue, aligns with late-May/June cooling-season urgency, and can still support IAQ/efficiency add-ons without making upsells the primary bet.

**Why this segment wins now**

Residential replacement demand is one of the stronger 2026 HVAC signals. ACHR News reports that residential HVAC demand appears to have bottomed, with replacement activity and improving existing-home sales offering the strongest near-term peaks. That matters because replacement buyers already have pain: old equipment, failures, comfort issues, repair-versus-replace decisions, and summer urgency.

Commercial maintenance contracts are attractive but usually slower. They create recurring revenue and business value, but a 30-day push may get you meetings more than signed work unless you already have warm commercial relationships.

IAQ/energy-efficiency upsells are useful, but as a standalone segment they are too dependent on existing traffic. They work best as an attachment to service calls and replacements, not as the main acquisition target.

**Evidence**

- The U.S. HVAC labor market remains tight: BLS projects HVACR mechanic/installer employment to grow 8% from 2024 to 2034, with about 40,100 openings per year. That supports demand, but also means capacity discipline matters. Source: BLS: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/installation-maintenance-and-repair/heating-air-conditioning-and-refrigeration-mechanics-and-installers.htm
- Replacement and repair are core demand drivers, and existing-system work is stronger than new construction in the current rate environment. Source: ACHR News 2026 outlook: https://www.achrnews.com/articles/165674-signs-of-a-turnaround-what-2026-holds-for-the-hvac-construction-market
- Residential energy pain is easy to message: EIA says space heating and air conditioning made up 52% of household energy use in 2020. Source: EIA: https://www.eia.gov/energyexplained/use-of-energy/homes.php
- Residential contractors are under cost pressure, and nearly all surveyed companies have raised prices recently. That means you need qualified leads, financing/payment framing, and tight quoting, not broad low-intent marketing. Source: Contracting Business / EGIA 2026 survey: https://www.contractingbusiness.com/industry-news/news/55373105/2026-hvac-survey-finds-rising-prices-and-ai-interest
- Be careful with tax-credit messaging: IRS 2025 Form 5695 instructions say energy efficient home improvement credits cannot be claimed for property placed in service after December 31, 2025. Do not lead with federal HVAC tax credits unless you verify a current local/state utility rebate. Source: IRS: https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i5695

**Main Risks**

- **Price resistance:** homeowners are sensitive to replacement quotes. Counter with good/better/best options, clear financing language, and repair-vs-replace math.
- **Lead quality:** generic "AC replacement" ads can waste money. Target older systems, no-cool calls, high repair estimates, poor airflow, high utility bills, and "second opinion" offers.
- **Capacity drag:** replacements can consume install time. Do not sell more than you can install cleanly.
- **Refrigerant/equipment confusion:** customers may hear conflicting claims about newer refrigerants and efficiency rules. Keep the message simple: availability, warranty, comfort, operating cost, and code-compliant installation.
- **Commercial opportunity cost:** you should not abandon commercial entirely. Keep a light outbound lane, but do not make it the 30-day focus.

**30-Day Plan**

1. **Define the offer this week:** "AC/heat pump replacement second opinion + same-week install slots." Include financing, warranty, and a repair-credit option if legal/financially workable.
2. **Target the right homeowners:** Prioritize homes with systems 12+ years old, recent no-cool calls, repeat repairs, high bills, weak airflow, or compressor/coil failures.
3. **Run two lead channels:** Use Google Local Services/Search for high-intent replacement terms, and reactivate your own database with SMS/email/calls to past repair and maintenance customers.
4. **Attach IAQ/efficiency, don't lead with it:** Every estimate should include optional add-ons: smart thermostat, filtration, duct sealing check, surge protection, maintenance plan, and airflow/static pressure review.
5. **Track only the numbers that matter:** Leads, booked estimates, close rate, average ticket, gross margin, install calendar fill, and source cost per sold job.

Bottom line: for the next 30 days, chase **residential replacement leads**, package them with efficiency/comfort options, and use the campaign to seed maintenance plans. Commercial maintenance should be your slower 90-day stability play, not the immediate sprint.
